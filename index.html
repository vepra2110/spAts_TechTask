A Tale of Two Cities

-Charles Dickens

This historical fiction novel, set in London and Paris before and during the French Revolution, explores themes of resurrection and transformation. It's one of the best-selling books of all time, with over 200 million copies sold

The Little Prince

-Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

A beloved children's fantasy novel that follows the adventures of a young prince who travels the universe, learning about love, friendship, and human nature. It has sold approximately 200 million copies worldwide

The Alchemist

-Paulo Coelho

This philosophical novel tells the story of a young shepherd's journey to find his personal legend. It explores themes of destiny, spirituality, and the importance of following one's dreams

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

-J.K. Rowling

The first book in the iconic Harry Potter series introduces readers to the magical world of Hogwarts and follows young wizard Harry Potter as he discovers his heritage and faces the dark wizard Voldemort

And Then There Were None

-Agatha Christie

A classic mystery novel in which ten strangers are lured to an isolated island and begin to die one by one, following the pattern of a nursery rhyme

Dream of the Red Chamber

-Cao Xueqin

This 18th-century Chinese novel is considered one of the greatest works of Chinese literature. It details the decline of a noble family and explores themes of love, societal expectations, and Buddhist philosophy

The Hobbit

-J.R.R. Tolkien

A fantasy adventure novel that follows Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit who joins a group of dwarves on a quest to reclaim their homeland from a dragon. It serves as a prelude to Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy

Alice's Adventures in Wonderland

-Lewis Carroll

This whimsical children's novel follows Alice as she falls down a rabbit hole into a fantastical world filled with peculiar anthropomorphic creatures. It's known for its playful use of logic and language
